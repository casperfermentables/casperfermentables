E-COMMERCE SOLUTIONS

One of the core components for both of our sites is making it easy for clients to find a provider in their area, add a payment method, and start scheduling. Payments are handled using a custom Braintree integration, which has handled over $3 million in sales over the past three years. Once a client has an attached payment method, they can purchase packages for coaches, monitor their order status and use the package for lesson credits. Providers receive notifications about new bookings and can use their backend to schedule lessons from a booking. Our system also monitors and tracks providers receiving payment for lessons. The booking platform was one of the first features we developed, and I've worked with numerous developers over the years to improve it and expand it's functionality.

RECURRING BILLING AND SCHEDULING

Last Fall I led an a development initiative to make one of the biggest changes to our scheduling platform since it was first set up. We created an option that allowed for providers to charge clients on a per-lesson basis. This involved a significant re-engineering of our upfront order-based system, but providing this option on the front-end doubled our online conversions over the fall and winter. We've removed it for our busy seasons, since many providers get filled up quickly during the spring and summer and prefer customer with pre-purchased packages during this time. However the ability to add pay-per-session pricing is now an easy toggle and can even be enabled on a per client basis for continued engagement after completing an initial package.

SALESFORCE INTEGRATIONS

Behind the scenes, MyTennisLessons and FitnessTrainer's operations rely on Salesforce to keep track of our user-base and the health of our business. Our Salesforce integration lets our team stay on top of potential clients, track instructor applications, and provides a data-hub for our marketing integrations. In 2015 we integrated with Heroku Connect, making our Salesforce integration incredibly easy to manage and allowing our Salesforce data to become a powerful tool for new data-driven applications.

CUSTOM SEO ENGINE

The first version of our SEO engine was as bootstrapped as it gets. We had a Wordpress template that we copied and pasted for each new city. Viola, engine made. But over time as we on-boarded more providers and added new cities, it became difficult to manage the hundreds of pages. So in 2013 I led an initiative to create a dynamic SEO-engine. Important things we had to consider for an SEO engine were defaults and overrides for content and seo tags, CDN caching for maximum performance, and ease-of-use by end users. Our first version was actually a static site generator, for ultimate page-load speed, but ultimately it was difficult to maintain and allow marketers easy access, so we soon switched over to a Rails-based solution. That solution now powers thousands of first-page ranked landing pages for tennis lessons and personal trainers across the country, all with minimal developer involvement.

DYNAMIC GEO-SEARCH RESULTS

Every e-commerce site needs a strong search page to easily connect users with the products that matter to them. We use Algolia to index all of our service-providers and deliver a quick and responsive search experience. The initial implementation was set in place by our Lead Developer in 2014, Luke Wendling, but since then I've used that framework to power email campaigns, custom recommendation pages, and admin-search interfaces. Most recently I implemented a custom node script that can be used in Zapier to find the closest 3 providers, which can then be used in marketing emails or other Zaps. Algolia recently released a component library for search results, Instantsearch.js, that I've been testing on side projects and hope to include on the MyTennisLessons and FitnessTrainer platform soon for a next-level search interface.

EMAIL MARKETING SOLUTIONS

Over the past five years, email has played an important role in engaging our client and provider networks. One of the biggest challenges we've faced is building dynamic drip campaigns that factor in the user's actions on the website, sales person's interactions with the user, and the lifecycle stage of users in our signup process. Using Salesforce as a datasource for our marketing integrations has allowed us to bridge the gap between user autonomous actions and sales-person interactions. Our marketing solutions have ranged from flat-out manual email sending, custom Rails drip mailers, Customer.io, Mailchimp, Hubspot, and Act-On, in a search to try and find the perfect balance of ease-of-access by marketing end-users and effective segment targeting. Today I'm happy to say we've found our best balance yet by using SendWithUs for amazingly easy to access templates that work for in-app emails, drip campaigns, and marketing blasts. This allows us to change, test, and grow our email templates, without costly code changes.

ADMIN USER INTERFACES

I've spoken with many people over the years looking to setup or currently running a marketplace, and almost always I have to remind people about one of the most important users of the system..admins. A lot of focus goes into the service provider and recipient user's experiences. But invariably any marketplace service is going to have to play some sort of role in-between these two sides of the service. Making sure that admin users can easily access important user data, see reports to understand the health of the business, and provide quick and efficient support is essential to providing a best-in-class service. Most of our admin interfaces are built off of ActiveAdmin, which is where I first learned to use Ruby on Rails, and allow basic CRUD operations and a nice separation of concerns for any custom actions. More and more though we've been moving admin functionality into Saleforce, and syncing the data using a combination of Heroku Connect and custom webhooks with Zapier.
